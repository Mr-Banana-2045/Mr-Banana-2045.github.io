In the concepts of information technology, "computer networks" or "data network" is called "network" for short, which means that computers are tools and objects that share information in an understandable way. At a higher and conceptual level, data networks include nodes that computers and digital devices can transfer messages between nodes through links and physical communications (wired and wireless). Network computer devices that are the origin and termination of data in the network are called network nodes or network nodes. Nodes can include personal computers, telephones, servers, and network hardware equipment. It can be said that two devices are networked when it is possible to exchange data from one device to another, either directly or indirectly. A wide range of applications and services exist and are used in a computer network. Such as the World Wide Web, the Internet, digital audio and video services, shared applications and storage services, printers and fax machines, e-mail messaging programs and chat services, and many others. Below are some of the important historical events in the field of networking and improvements and developments in computer networks: In the late 1950s, the first computer network was used in the SAGE (Semi-Automatic Ground Environment) system, which was a large computer system for processing and analyzing data received from US radar sites. The system produced an integrated image of the airspace over a wide area, used by the North American Aerospace Defense Command (NORAD). In 1959, Anatoly Ivankovich Kitov presented to the Central Committee of the Communist Party of the Soviet Union a proposal to create a detailed plan to reorganize the control of the Soviet military forces and the Soviet economic system based on a network of computing centers.
